## The Doctrine of Sympathy & Antipathy

Culpeper structured the entire *English Physician* around a Galenic-astrological logic: **every herb is governed by a planet**, every disease ruled by another, and the physick succeeds when the herb is chosen by sympathy (same planet, like for like) or by **antipathy** (the opposing planet, contrary to the cause).

### The four humours

### The opposition protocol

Diseases of the Hot and Dry (choleric fevers, inflammations, jaundice, plague-spots, frenzies) are **Martial**. They are answered by herbs of **Venus** (mallows, violets, plantain, roses) and of the **Moon** (lettuce, water lily, purslane, poppies) — the cool, moist contraries.

Diseases of the Cold and Moist (phlegmatic catarrhs, dropsies, palsies, rheums, agues from cold) are **Lunar**. They are met by **Saturn** (which dries by contraction) and by **Mars** (which burns the gross humour) — though Culpeper repeatedly warns the practitioner to dose these by gentle stages.

The crossings of **Jupiter and Mercury** form a second axis: Mercury's variable, mixed quality answers the over-warmth and excess of sanguine matter, and Jupiter's temperate moisture corrects the dry, scattering nature of Mercury's wind, palsy, and disordered nerves.

### How to use this codex

1. **By sympathy:** select a planet, sign, body part, or condition — you will see all herbs of that nature.
2. **By antipathy:** select a planet, then enable *Opposition mode*. The codex will show the herbs whose nature is contrary, and which Culpeper would prescribe to balance the offending humour.
