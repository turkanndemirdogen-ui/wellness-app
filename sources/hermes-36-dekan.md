## The doctrine of sympathy in the Hermetic prescription

The *Sacred Book of Hermes to Asclepius* (Ἱερὰ Βίβλος, often abbreviated *Liber Sacer*) is preserved chiefly in Paris, Bibliothèque nationale, gr. 2419 and related Byzantine codices, and was edited from these by Charles-Émile Ruelle in 1908. André-Jean Festugière, in volume I of *La révélation d'Hermès Trismégiste* (1944), set the text in the wider corpus of Greco-Egyptian astrological medicine — the *iatromathēmatika*.

### The amulet construction

For each decan the text supplies a fixed instruction-set:

1. engrave the decan's figure on a particular stone,
2. place the prescribed plant beneath the engraved gem,
3. enclose stone and plant together in a wrapper (linen, leather, or metal foil),
4. wear the amulet against the afflicted region,
5. abstain from the prohibited food.

The closing sentence of the proem makes the doctrine explicit: *"For without this decanic disposition, nothing is born, since the All is contained in it"* (Ruelle ed., col. 1).

### Why these stones and these plants?

Three principles, repeatedly visible in the table, govern the choice:

**1. The doctrine of signatures (homological sympathy).** Many materia are chosen because their form mirrors the organ. Pentadactyl — *cinquefoil* — five-fingered, heals the hand. *Bostrychite*, the curled stone, heals the inner ear. *Leontopodion* (lion's-foot) heals the heart that beats in the Lion's mansion. *Dendrite*'s branching veins mirror the intestinal coils.

**2. The doctrine of cosmic affinity (astral sympathy).** A planet imprints its virtue on the metals and stones generated in the earth under its sign. Iron answers to Mars, gold to the Sun, silver to the Moon — and the decan, ruled by one of the seven planets in Chaldean order (Saturn-Jupiter-Mars-Sun-Venus-Mercury-Moon), draws down that virtue through its consonant materia. The hematite of the first Scorpio decan is a Martial stone for a Martial decan governing a Martial organ.

**3. The pharmacological tradition.** Independent of these symbolic registers, the actual herbs — plantain, dittany, vervain, mugwort, liquorice, asarabacca — were the standard Greco-Roman pharmacopeia of Dioscorides and Pliny. The decanic system encodes a working herbalism within a cosmological frame.

### The dietary taboos

The forbidden foods are not arbitrary. They cluster around eels and moray (cold, slimy, dampening to the joints), boar and pork (heavy and bilious), torpedo-rays (numbing — counter to the joint-loosening intent), beans (pneuma-clouding, per Pythagorean and Egyptian taboo), and certain birds (whose flesh was thought to carry sky-borne miasms). In every case the prohibition reinforces the talisman by removing humoural antagonists from the patient's diet.

### A note on the metals

The *Sacred Book* itself does not name metals — it names stones. The metals shown in this dashboard are derived from the decan's planetary ruler in the Chaldean order, by the standard planet-metal correspondences attested in Greco-Egyptian alchemy and in the *Picatrix*: Saturn-lead, Jupiter-tin, Mars-iron, Sun-gold, Venus-copper, Mercury-quicksilver, Moon-silver. This rounds the system out as the later Hermetic tradition received it.
